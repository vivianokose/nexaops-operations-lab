# ADR-0002: Personal Engineering Principles

## Status
Accepted, revisable

## Principles I will hold myself to

1. **Reproducibility over speed.** A lab that works only on my machine is not a lab, it is an anecdote.
2. **Document the failure, not just the success.** The post-mortem note is more valuable than the screenshot of the green check.
3. **Tear down what I spin up.** Every cloud lab has a teardown step. No exceptions.
4. **Read the man page before the Stack Overflow answer.** The man page is the source of truth. Stack Overflow is the source of bias.
5. **Commit small, commit often, commit in English.** Future me reads commit messages. Future me deserves clarity.
6. **One change at a time.** When something breaks, I changed too many things at once.
7. **Ask for help after 30 minutes of stuck, not 3.** And after 3 hours, write the question down and walk away.
8. **My screenshots are evidence, not decoration.** Name them. Caption them. Use them in writeups.
